Why nuclear might actually be the green choice

Shutting down factories to "go green" can just move the pollution somewhere else. There's a smarter way.

Vigilia·Plain edition·19 June 2026·1 min read

A lot of people hear the word "nuclear" and immediately think danger. But if what you actually want is loads of clean electricity that keeps flowing even when the wind drops and the sun goes down, nuclear turns out to be one of the best tools we have. Low-carbon, reliable, and something Europe can build for itself.

Here's the trap worth spotting. "Going green" by shutting down European factories doesn't save the planet. It just ships the pollution off to China and sends the jobs along with it, while your energy bill climbs at home. That's not clean. It's just poorer, with better branding.

The smarter version isn't complicated: build nuclear, add wind and solar where they actually make sense, connect the power grids across countries so electricity can flow to where it's needed, and be honest about what each choice really costs in money and jobs.

What this means for you is simple. You shouldn't have to choose between a livable climate and a livable life. Anyone who tells you the only way to be green is to be poorer is selling you a worse deal than the one that's actually on the table.

The goal is a Europe that's clean and strong, not poor and pure. We can absolutely do both. We just have to stop pretending decline is a virtue.
